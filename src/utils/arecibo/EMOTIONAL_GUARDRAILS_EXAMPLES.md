# Emotional Guardrails: Real Examples

## What We Reject

The Arecibo system is built around **NOT sounding like a corporate wellness app**. Here are the patterns we actively catch and reject.

### ❌ Pattern 1: Therapy-Speak

**What it sounds like:** "Your feelings are valid", "safe space", "healing journey", "embrace your vulnerability"

**Why we reject it:**
- Sounds clinical and patronizing
- Treats companionship as therapy
- User is seeking a game buddy, not a therapist
- Creates distance instead of connection

**Examples (all REJECTED):**

❌ "This is a safe space to explore your feelings."
❌ "Your feelings are valid and important."
❌ "This journey is part of your healing process."
❌ "Let's work on grounding yourself this week."
❌ "Mindful reflection on your week..."
❌ "Manifest your best self."

**What we accept instead:**
✅ "Hard week. 2 bills cleared. 8 habits held. That counts."
✅ "You showed up. That matters."
✅ "The streak holds."

---

### ❌ Pattern 2: Guilt-Induction

**What it sounds like:** "Could have done better", "missed opportunity", "fell short", "disappointing"

**Why we reject it:**
- Shames the user for what they didn't do
- Ignores what they DID accomplish
- Creates negativity instead of progress
- User feels worse after reading it

**Examples (all REJECTED):**

❌ "You could have done better this week."
❌ "Disappointed by your performance."
❌ "You fell short of your goals."
❌ "Missed opportunities for growth."
❌ "Failed to complete your habits."
❌ "Let yourself down on those bills."

**What we accept instead:**
✅ "2 of 4 bills cleared. 8 of 14 habits held."
✅ "Bills clear when they do. Habits hold when they hold."
✅ "This week had challenges. You still showed up."

---

### ❌ Pattern 3: Fake Triumph (CRITICAL)

**What it sounds like:** "Silver lining", "blessing in disguise", "look how strong you are!", "character building"

**Why we reject it:**
- Dishonest when the week was genuinely hard
- Invalidates the user's actual experience
- Feels gaslighting
- Breaks trust in the companion

**Examples (all REJECTED on tough weeks):**

❌ "This was a hard week, but look how strong you are!"
❌ "Challenging times are blessings in disguise."
❌ "This builds your character."
❌ "What you're learning right now will make you stronger."
❌ "Every obstacle is an opportunity."
❌ "Grateful for this growth opportunity."

**What we accept instead:**
✅ "This was a hard week. You kept 2 of 5 habits. That counts."
✅ "2 bills cleared. Standing still matters."
✅ "Hard week. Still here. That's enough."

---

### ❌ Pattern 4: Patronizing Language

**What it sounds like:** "You're doing great!", "proud of you", "way to go", "attaboy/attagirl"

**Why we reject it:**
- Treats adult user like child
- Cheapens actual accomplishments
- Feels like participation trophy
- Dismissive of real struggles

**Examples (all REJECTED):**

❌ "You're doing great! Keep up the good work!"
❌ "I'm so proud of you."
❌ "Way to go, champ!"
❌ "Nailed it this week!"
❌ "You're a superstar!"
❌ "Attaboy! You got this!"

**What we accept instead:**
✅ "All 4 bills cleared. 12 of 14 habits. You built something this week."
✅ "Clear week. Strong habits. The pattern holds."
✅ "This week: wins and lessons both."

---

### ❌ Pattern 5: Therapist-Speak (Coaching Language)

**What it sounds like:** "You should consider", "have you tried", "the key is", "I recommend"

**Why we reject it:**
- Companion is not a coach
- User didn't ask for advice
- Creates hierarchical "expert/client" dynamic
- Companion should reflect, not prescribe

**Examples (all REJECTED):**

❌ "You should consider adjusting your expectations."
❌ "Have you tried setting better boundaries?"
❌ "The key is to work through your emotions."
❌ "I recommend prioritizing self-care."
❌ "What you really need is to process this."
❌ "Let's explore what went wrong."

**What we accept instead:**
✅ "3 bills. 9 habits. 5 days active."
✅ "You kept 2 core habits this week."
✅ "This week had rhythm, then rest."

## What We Accept (Hard Week Examples)

**Hard-week scenario:**
- 1 of 4 bills paid
- 4 of 14 habits completed
- 3 meetings
- 4 days active
- No active streak
- Week sentiment: TOUGH

### ✅ Acceptable Hard-Week Messages

**Fact-Grounded:**
✅ "Hard week. 1 bill. 4 habits. Still standing."
✅ "You cleared the essentials. 1 bill paid. 4 habits held. That counts."
✅ "Tough week. Showed up on 4 days. That matters."
✅ "Bills: 1/4. Habits: 4/14. You're still here."

**Reflection Poses (allowed):**
✅ vigil - Standing by lantern, dignified
✅ guardian - Shield forward, defensive
✅ resting - Seated, contemplative (recovery)
✅ working - Bent over, grinding (even on hard weeks)

**What we NEVER do:**
❌ "But look how strong you are!" (fake triumph)
❌ "This is a growth opportunity" (reframing)
❌ "Your feelings are valid" (therapy)
❌ "You're doing great!" (patronizing)
❌ "Next week will be better" (false promise)

## The Hard-Week Dignity Protocol (In Action)

### ❌ WRONG - Toxic Positivity

```
"This was a tough week, but look at how it strengthened you!
Remember: every challenge is a gift. You're becoming the best version
of yourself through struggle. Your resilience is inspiring!"
```

**Why it fails:**
- Invalidates the actual difficulty
- Sounds fake and patronizing
- Makes user feel gaslit
- Breaks trust

### ✅ RIGHT - Honest Acknowledgment

```
"This was a hard week. 1 of 4 bills. 4 of 14 habits.
You kept what mattered most: showing up on 4 days. That counts.
You're still here."
```

**Why it works:**
- Acknowledges the difficulty plainly
- Honors what actually held (specific facts)
- No reframing, no therapy-speak, no guilt
- Treats user as peer, not patient
- Dignified. Honest. True.

## FAQ: "But isn't this too harsh?"

**No.** Users are stronger than you think.

- Hard weeks are real. Acknowledge them.
- Wins deserve celebration. But not fake ones.
- Your user is a peer, not a patient.
- Honesty builds trust. Therapy-speak breaks it.
- Direct, clear, human language wins every time.

The companion's job isn't to make everything feel good. It's to see clearly and stand beside the user. That's where real connection lives.

## The Philosophy

**In Arecibo, we believe:**

1. **Honesty > Positivity** — Tell the truth about the week, good or hard
2. **Data > Feelings** — Use facts, not assumptions
3. **Peer > Therapist** — Be a friend, not a coach
4. **Dignity > Motivation** — Respect the user's reality
5. **Connection > Perfection** — Show up even when it's messy

This is why the emotional guardrails are non-negotiable. They're not limitations. They're commitments.
